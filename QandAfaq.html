Frequently Asked Questions

How is reflection of light useful in daily life? +
1. Mirrors for seeing ourselves
2. Rear-view and side mirrors in vehicles
3. Cameras and mobile phones
4. Reflectors on bicycles and roads
5. Solar cookers
6. Torches (flashlights)
7. Optical instruments
8. House lighting, etc.
What happens if the mirror is not straight or moves? +
The angles will be incorrect, and the experiment results will be inaccurate.
Why is the image formed by a plane mirror virtual? +
Because the reflected rays do not actually meet; they only appear to meet behind the mirror.
Why do we draw a normal line? +
The normal line helps us measure the angles of incidence and reflection accurately.
Does the image distance equal the object distance? +
Yes. In a plane mirror, image distance = object distance.
Why do we use pins in this experiment? +
Pins help find the exact direction of the reflected ray by aligning them with the image of the object pin.
Why do we mark the positions of the pins? +
So we can draw the ray diagram accurately on the paper after removing the physical pins.
Can we use more than two pins? +
Two are enough, but using more pins can increase accuracy by helping verify alignment.
How do we know the pin method experiment is correct? +
When the measured distance of the image from the mirror equals the measured distance of the object pin from the mirror.
What is the conjugate foci method? +
It is a method to find the focal length of a concave mirror by placing the object and screen at two different distances where they interchange positions and still form sharp images.
Why are two object positions used? +
Because for two positions, the object and screen distances interchange, confirming the conjugate relationship and improving accuracy.
If incident rays are parallel, where is the image formed? +
Object: At infinity.
Image: Formed exactly at the focus (F).
Screen: Yes, it can be focused on a screen because the image formed at the focus (F) is a real image.
