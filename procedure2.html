Procedure (2) Pin Method

1. Draw the surface line on the paper and place the mirror strip upright on the surface line.
2. Mark the object point and draw a line representing the incident ray.
3. Place the two object pins (P, Q) on the incident ray and put two search pins (R, S) in a straight line with the images (P', Q'). The images (P', Q') and two search pins (R, S) should be on the same straight line.
4. Remove the mirror and all pins.
5. Draw the reflected rays passing through the search pin points and the normal line.
6. Measure the angle of incidence and angle of reflection.
7. Check whether the angle of incidence and reflection are equal or not.


Precautions

  • The observer's eye must be in the plane of the rays and not too close to the mirror.
  • A hard-shape pencil must be used to get accurate angle measurements.
  • Parallax error must be avoided.
  • Search pins R and S must not be too close to one another.
